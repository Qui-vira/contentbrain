## Framework 1: The Challenge-Teach-Command Framework

**Source:** Extracted from @brandon_.marketing (1.2M plays), @dmitrihasideas (1.1M plays), Adam Erhart (205K views)

### Structure
1. **Challenge** -- Open with a bold contrarian statement that challenges the audience's current behavior
2. **Prove** -- Show specific evidence why their current approach fails (data, example, or personal story)
3. **Teach** -- Deliver the alternative framework in 3-5 actionable steps
4. **Command** -- End with a direct instruction, not a suggestion ("Do this now" not "you might want to try")

### Why It Works (Psychology)
- The challenge creates cognitive dissonance: "Wait, I'm doing this wrong?"
- Proof validates the dissonance: "Oh, they have evidence"
- Teaching resolves the dissonance: "Here's what to do instead"
- The command removes decision paralysis: "Just do this"
- This mirrors the therapeutic pattern of confrontation -> awareness -> action

### Real Example from Data
@brandon_.marketing: "Stop using these words" (1.2M plays, 78K likes, 50.6K saves)
- Challenge: "Stop using these words" (with X overlay)
- Prove: Shows generic words vs specific alternatives side by side
- Teach: Each slide swaps a weak word for a powerful word
- Command: "SAVE this to use them later"

### @big_quiv Adaptation for Crypto/Web3
**TikTok script:**
- Challenge: "Stop saying 'DYOR' without teaching people HOW to research"
- Prove: "90% of 'DYOR' advice is just people protecting themselves from liability. Here's what real research looks like."
- Teach: 3 specific on-chain tools + what to look for in each
- Command: "Screenshot this. Use it before your next trade."

**Instagram carousel:**
- Slide 1: "Stop calling yourself a trader if you can't answer these 3 questions"
- Slides 2-4: Each question + why it matters + what the answer reveals
- Slide 5: "Now go back to your last 10 trades and check"

## Framework 2: The ABT (And-But-Therefore) Content Framework

**Source:** Extracted from @dmitrihasideas (1.2M plays -- he explicitly teaches this), Kallaway (2.3M views on storytelling), @xoxothatfacelesschick (69L/23C on IG)

### Structure
1. **AND** -- Set the scene. Establish the world the audience lives in. (Agreement)
2. **BUT** -- Introduce the conflict. Something is wrong, broken, or missing. (Tension)
3. **THEREFORE** -- Deliver the resolution. The insight, framework, or action step. (Resolution)

### Why It Works (Psychology)
- AND creates rapport: "Yes, I live in that world too"
- BUT creates tension: "Oh no, something is off"
- THEREFORE creates relief: "Thank god, here's the answer"
- This is the fundamental structure of every story ever told. It's hardwired into how the brain processes information. Content that follows ABT is neurologically stickier than content that follows "Here are 5 tips."

### Real Example from Data
@dmitrihasideas: "there IS A DISTINCT FORMULA to content that CONVERTS and you're literally watching it" (160K plays)
- AND: "You're creating content every day..."
- BUT: "...but nobody is converting. Your views don't become followers, your followers don't become customers."
- THEREFORE: "Because you're missing the ABT structure. Here's how to use it in every single piece of content."

@xoxothatfacelesschick: "I cheated. Not on him. On me." (69L/23C -- highest IG engagement)
- AND: "For months I said 'It's fine. This is what sells.'"
- BUT: "I edited out every part of me that felt inconvenient."
- THEREFORE: "The version of you that converts isn't the safe version. It's the real one."

### @big_quiv Adaptation
**X/Twitter thread:**
- AND: "Everyone in crypto talks about finding the next 100x."
- BUT: "Nobody talks about the 47 projects they ape'd into that went to zero first."
- THEREFORE: "Here are the 3 filters I run before I touch ANY new token. Saved me $200K+ in 2025 alone."

**TikTok:**

- BUT: "You still lose money on every trade because you enter late and exit scared."
- THEREFORE: "Stop following calls. Start reading charts. Here's the 3-candle setup I use for every entry."

## Framework 3: The Reciprocity Bomb Framework

**Source:** Extracted from @hackeddonttrust (1.7M plays, 103K saves), Nicolas Cole (multiple 30-100K videos), Alex Hormozi (520K views)

### Structure
1. **Over-deliver first** -- Give away something that feels like it should be paid content
2. **Frame the gift** -- Explicitly say "this is free" or "yours today" or "I usually charge for this"
3. **Create debt** -- The audience feels they owe you attention, a follow, or a purchase
4. **Offer the ladder** -- Present a logical next step (free > community > paid)

### Why It Works (Psychology)
- Reciprocity is the most powerful social trigger. When someone gives us something, we feel obligated to return the favor.
- The framing ("I usually charge for this") amplifies the perceived value
- The "ladder" converts the reciprocity into action without feeling transactional
- Robert Cialdini's research shows reciprocity triggers compliance rates 2-3x higher than direct asks

### Real Example from Data
@hackeddonttrust: "This is one of my favorite social media strategies to implement for clients - yours free today!" (1.7M plays, 153.9K likes, 103K saves)
- Over-deliver: Full strategy breakdown that agencies charge for
- Frame: "Yours free today!" + "I use MANY more strategies for private clients"
- Debt: 103K saves = 103K people who feel they received value
- Ladder: Implied -- "more strategies for private clients" = DM me for paid work

Alex Hormozi: Every YouTube video follows this pattern
- Over-deliver: "1.2M Followers in 6 Months" -- gives the entire system
- Frame: "Free scaling roadmap" in every description
- Debt: 23K likes, 584 comments on a strategy video
- Ladder: Free roadmap -> Skool community -> $1M+ advisory

**YouTube:**
- Record "The Exact System I Use to Find 10x Altcoins Before CT" (full breakdown, nothing held back)
- Frame: "This is the research process I charge consulting clients $5K for. Yours for free."
- CTA: "Download the full checklist" (lead magnet) -> "Join The Krib for weekly alpha" -> "Book a 1:1 strategy call"

**TikTok:**

- End: "Comment SETUP for the full spreadsheet tracker I use"

## Framework 4: The Identity Mirror Framework

**Source:** Extracted from @roysdigitalmedia (2M plays), @stephikeh (1.3M plays, 267K likes), @xoxothatfacelesschick (69L/23C IG)

### Structure
1. **Mirror** -- Describe the audience's current situation so accurately they feel seen
2. **Agitate** -- Name the specific frustration they haven't articulated yet
3. **Redefine** -- Offer a new identity or label that resolves the frustration
4. **Path** -- Show them the first step toward that new identity

### Why It Works (Psychology)
- People don't buy products or follow accounts. They buy IDENTITIES. "I'm a trader" is an identity. "I'm a builder, not a talker" is an identity.
- When you mirror someone's reality perfectly, they feel understood. That feeling creates trust faster than any credibility claim.
- The identity redefinition creates aspiration without condescension. You're not saying "you're wrong." You're saying "you're not who you think you are -- you're actually something better."

### Real Example from Data
@roysdigitalmedia: "If your socials aren't giving main character energy, it might be time to change that" (2M plays, 52.4K likes)
- Mirror: "Your socials aren't giving main character energy"
- Agitate: "Investing now means you're not playing catch-up later (or wishing you started sooner)"
- Redefine: "We did it, and now we're building the dream -- your brand should be too"
- Path: Implied -- their agency is the bridge

@stephikeh: "Stop wasting time doing nothing. Learn a high income skill like ghostwriting" (1.3M plays, 267K likes)
- Mirror: "You're wasting time" (audience knows this is true)
- Agitate: "Saving alone will not make you wealthy"
- Redefine: "You can be someone who earns in dollars from writing"
- Path: "Send me a DM to start"

- Agitate: "But your portfolio is still red. Because consuming alpha is not the same as executing strategy."
- Redefine: "You're not a degen. You're an undisciplined strategist. There's a difference."

## Framework 5: The Specificity Stack Framework

**Source:** Extracted from Alex Hormozi (multiple videos totaling 2M+ views), Copy That! (1.13M views), @gushmedia.net (26K plays)

### Structure
1. **Generic statement** -- Start with what everyone says (the cliche)
2. **Specificity layer 1** -- Add a concrete number or metric
3. **Specificity layer 2** -- Add a named tool, system, or person
4. **Specificity layer 3** -- Add a time frame or condition
5. **Contrast** -- Show the generic version vs the specific version side by side

### Why It Works (Psychology)
- Specificity is the #1 trust signal in copywriting. "I made $100K" is 10x more believable than "I made a lot of money."
- Each layer of specificity removes a layer of skepticism
- The contrast at the end makes the lesson visceral and memorable
- From the dataset: posts with 3+ specific details in the hook outperform vague posts by 10-15x

### Real Example from Data
@gushmedia.net: "3 shifts that double conversions" (26K plays)
- Generic: "Write better copy"
- Layer 1: "3 shifts" (number)
- Layer 2: "Features to Feelings" (named transformation)
- Layer 3: "'Handmade ceramic mug' becomes 'The mug that makes Monday feel less Monday'" (specific before/after)
- Contrast: Side-by-side comparison of generic vs specific copy

Copy That!: "40 Years of Pro Copywriting Knowledge in 5 Hours" (1.13M views)
- Generic: "Learn copywriting"
- Layer 1: "40 years" (timeframe compressed)
- Layer 2: "Pro" (quality level)
- Layer 3: "5 hours" (investment required)
- Contrast: 40 years of work condensed into 5 hours = extreme value proposition

### @big_quiv Adaptation
**Hook writing process:**
1. Start generic: "How to trade crypto"
2. Add number: "The 3-step process I use before every crypto trade"
3. Add tool: "The 3-step TradingView process I use before every crypto trade"
4. Add condition: "The 3-step TradingView process I use before every crypto trade (even in a bear market)"
5. Contrast: "Most traders check Twitter. I check these 3 indicators. Here's the difference in my P&L."

## Framework 6: The Loop Engine Framework

**Source:** Extracted from @devin.jatho (408K plays -- explicitly teaches this), @mysocialsidekick (2.3M plays)

### Structure
1. **Open with resolution** -- Start with the end result or the conclusion
2. **Rewind to problem** -- "But let me show you how I got here"
3. **Walk through process** -- 3-5 rapid steps
4. **Return to resolution** -- End exactly where you started
5. **No CTA** -- The loop IS the CTA (viewer watches again)

### Why It Works (Psychology)
- Loop videos exploit the Zeigarnik effect: the brain wants to complete unfinished sequences
- Starting at the end creates a "wait, how did they get there?" response
- The absence of a CTA feels generous and non-transactional
- TikTok and Instagram algorithms reward watch time above all other metrics. A 30-second video watched 3x = 90 seconds of watch time = algorithmic distribution
- @devin.jatho specifically teaches: "STOP using CTAs. Make loop videos instead. Your watch time will 3x."

### Real Example from Data
@devin.jatho: "STOP Using CTA's Like This" (408K plays, 29.9K likes, 18.2K saves)
- Resolution: Shows final analytics dashboard with high retention
- Rewind: "Here's what most people do wrong" (shows weak CTA)
- Process: "Instead, structure your video so the ending leads back to the beginning"
- Return: Cuts back to the analytics dashboard
- Effect: 18.2K saves -- people save loop tutorials because they want to study the technique

### @big_quiv Adaptation
**TikTok trading content:**
- Open: Show the completed trade result (+$4,200)
- Rewind: "24 hours ago, I saw this setup on the chart"
- Process: Show entry, stop loss placement, target setting
- Return: "And that's how I ended up here" (cuts back to +$4,200)
- Loop: Viewer watches again to study the entry

**Instagram Reel:**
- Open: Screenshot of portfolio growth
- Rewind: "3 months ago this account was at $0"
- Process: Show the 5 strategies applied week by week
- Return: Portfolio growth screenshot again
- No CTA: The content drives saves and shares naturally

## Framework 7: The Pain-Specificity-System Framework

**Source:** Extracted from @megankachigan (12L/2C IG), @petsiness (24L/32C IG -- highest comment count in IG dataset), @samuel.gohighlevel (6L/0C IG), Alex Cattoni (307K views)

### Structure
1. **Name the specific pain** -- Not "business is hard" but "your sales slowed down and you're chasing more traffic"
2. **Diagnose the root cause** -- "The problem isn't reach. It's your message."
3. **Introduce a named system** -- "This is what I call the _____ Method"
4. **Walk through the system** -- 3-5 steps with specific examples
5. **Show the transformation** -- Before/after with metrics

### Why It Works (Psychology)
- Specific pain creates immediate recognition ("that's ME")
- Root cause diagnosis positions you as the expert (you see what they can't)
- A named system creates perceived intellectual property (it feels proprietary and valuable)
- The transformation provides social proof without requiring testimonials
- This is the #1 framework used by creators selling high-ticket services ($2K-$10K+)

### Real Example from Data
@petsiness: "When sales slow down, most brands react the same way" (24L/32C -- 32 comments is exceptionally high for IG in this dataset)
- Pain: "Sales slow down"
- Misguided response: "They chase more traffic. More ads. More reach."
- Root cause: "If your message doesn't connect, more traffic just means more people leaving without buying"
- System: "The real leverage isn't getting more people to your site. It's making the words on your site impossible to ignore."
- Transformation: Implied -- work with us and your message will convert

@megankachigan: "Conversion copywriting is missing the mark" (12L/2C)
- Pain: "Brands optimize for conversions"
- Root cause: "The healthiest businesses optimize for a different metric: client retention"
- System: Retention-first copywriting approach
- Transformation: "When someone buys once and disappears, something in the marketing promised more than the product delivered"

## Framework 8: The Contrast Bridge Framework

**Source:** Extracted from @gushmedia.net (26K plays), @filipnphilip (4L/0C IG), Joanna Wiebe / Copyhackers (multiple YouTube videos)

### Structure
1. **Show the weak version** -- Quote or display the generic/bad copy verbatim
2. **Name why it fails** -- "This sounds professional but says nothing"
3. **Show the strong version** -- Rewrite it with specificity and emotion
4. **Name why it works** -- "This works because it speaks to the pain, not the feature"
5. **Give the rule** -- Extract a reusable principle the audience can apply themselves

### Why It Works (Psychology)
- Before/after comparisons are the most shareable content format across all platforms
- Seeing the "bad" version first makes the audience self-audit ("Oh no, I write like that")
- The rewrite proves competence in real-time (you're not just teaching -- you're demonstrating)
- The extracted rule gives the audience a tool, not just an example

### Real Example from Data
@gushmedia.net: "3 shifts that double conversions" (26K plays, 2.2K likes, 1.6K saves)
- Weak: "Handmade ceramic mug"
- Why it fails: Features describe. Feelings sell.
- Strong: "The mug that makes Monday feel less Monday"
- Why it works: Connects to an emotional state, not a product attribute
- Rule: Features -> Feelings. Always translate the feature into the feeling it creates.

@filipnphilip: "Talking nicely but saying nothing" (4L/0C IG)
- Weak: "We provide innovative solutions" / "We help businesses grow" / "Customer focused approach"
- Why it fails: "These sentences sound good but they do not explain anything"
- Strong: Specific, clear value propositions
- Rule: If you can swap your company name with a competitor's and the sentence still works, it says nothing.

### @big_quiv Adaptation
**Instagram carousel (trading education):**
- Slide 1: "Your trading plan probably says this"
- Slide 2: Weak version: "I will manage risk carefully" / "I will take profits at the right time"
- Slide 3: "This means nothing. You'll ignore it when emotions hit."
- Slide 4: Strong version: "Max 2% of account per trade. If RSI > 70, no new longs. Take 50% at 2R, trail the rest."
- Slide 5: "Specificity is discipline. Vague rules are broken rules."
- Slide 6: The Rule: "If your trading plan could apply to any trader, it won't protect YOU."

## Framework 9: The Earned Authority Framework

**Source:** Extracted from Nicolas Cole (multiple 30-100K videos), Dakota Robertson (49K views), @courtney..johnson (370K plays)

### Structure
1. **Admit what you didn't know** -- "When I started, I had no idea what I was doing"
2. **Show the messy middle** -- Specific failures, losses, embarrassments
3. **Name the turning point** -- One specific moment, decision, or insight that changed everything
4. **Deliver the framework** -- The system you built from the failure
5. **Prove it works** -- Current results with specific numbers

### Why It Works (Psychology)
- Vulnerability creates trust faster than credentials
- The "messy middle" is relatable -- everyone has experienced confusion and failure
- The turning point creates a narrative arc (every good story has a pivot moment)
- The framework feels earned, not theoretical -- "I built this from pain, not from a textbook"
- Current results close the loop -- "and it actually worked"

### Real Example from Data
Nicolas Cole: "How I Would Make My First $100,000/Yr As A Writer (Starting Over)" (39.7K views)
- Admit: "When I was 23, I had just graduated college with a degree in Fiction Writing and had no idea how to make money as a writer"
- Messy middle: Years of learning through trial and error
- Turning point: Discovery of digital writing and ghostwriting as business model
- Framework: Step-by-step system for building a writing income
- Proof: "Today, I run a portfolio of writing businesses that does over $5,000,000 in annual revenue"

@courtney..johnson: "Side hustle idea: ghostwriting on LinkedIn" (370K plays, 22.5K likes)
- Admit: Started as a side hustle, not a grand plan
- Messy middle: Learning process of ghostwriting for CEOs
- Turning point: Realized LinkedIn ghostwriting was underserved
- Framework: How to position yourself, find clients, deliver
- Proof: "I've had this as a side hustle for years. I learn so much from writing"

## Quick Reference: Framework Selection Guide

| Situation | Best Framework | Why |
|-----------|---------------|-----|
| Audience is doing something wrong | Challenge-Teach-Command | Direct confrontation + solution |
| Need to tell a story that teaches | ABT (And-But-Therefore) | Neurologically sticky narrative |
| Want maximum saves/shares | Reciprocity Bomb | Over-delivering triggers reciprocity |
| Building community/tribe | Identity Mirror | People follow identities, not advice |
| Writing hooks or headlines | Specificity Stack | Each layer removes skepticism |
| Short-form video (TikTok/Reels) | Loop Engine | Maximizes watch time and algorithm boost |
| Selling high-ticket service | Pain-Specificity-System | Names the pain precisely, offers proprietary solution |
| Teaching copywriting/positioning | Contrast Bridge | Before/after is the most shareable format |
| Building personal brand | Earned Authority | Vulnerability + results = trust |
