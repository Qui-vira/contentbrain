## POSTING FORMULA

The most common repeatable content creation formula across all 72 creators:

### 1. Hook (First Line)
The dominant hook style is the **Bold Claim + Immediate Payoff** pattern. 3 variations dominate:

- **"This is [dramatic thing]"** — States what the viewer is about to see with urgency.
  - "This is How To Get 3D Tracking Title Cards On Your Phone!" (@__diditee__)
  - "This is the first AI model that can generate videos endlessly" (@drcintas)
  - "This is the low to high quality effect" (@tapewarp.ai)

- **"[Shocking result/number] + how"** — Leads with an outcome, then promises the method.
  - "A 22-year-old dropout is making $700,000/year from videos people fall asleep to" (@nivedan.ai)
  - "Last month I made $114,224 across my 22 channels" (@scottsmithyta)
  - "Claude Code just became free" (@thevibefounder)

- **Short provocative statement (under 10 words)** — Punchy, curiosity-driven one-liners.
  - "Its getting scary good" (@kamranzah1d)
  - "Life is unfair. Use your unfair advantages." (@im_roy_lee)
  - "Claude is on top of the game" (@steveofallstreets)

### 2. Body (Middle Structure)
Two dominant body formats:

- **Numbered step-by-step walkthrough** (used in ~35% of posts) — Uses emoji numbers (1-7 steps), each step is 1-2 sentences max. Reads like a recipe. Example: @__diditee__ walks through 7 steps with clear tool names and actions at each step.

- **Problem-Solution narrative** (used in ~25% of posts) — Opens with a pain point ("Most people waste hours..."), pivots to the solution ("Here's the trick..."), then delivers the method. Example: @timkoda_ opens with "Your AI images look like everyone else's. Here's why" then delivers the LoRA training system.

### 3. Close (CTA Style)
The overwhelming CTA pattern is the **Comment Keyword Trigger** — used in **42 out of 86 posts (49%)**:

- "Comment BANANA to get my 3D reference pack" (@__diditee__)
- "Comment STACK to get my Claude Code guide" (@timkoda_)
- "Comment skills for a link to claude skills" (@albert.olgaard)
- "Comment REALISM to get the tool link + tutorial" (@ineffable_ai22)
- "Comment AI for my full blueprint" (@kamranzah1d)

Secondary CTA patterns:
- **"Follow for more"** — Simple follow ask, used in ~15% of posts
- **"Save this"** — Targeting the save metric, used in ~8% of posts
- **"Link in bio" / "DM me"** — Direct funnel push, used in ~10% of posts

## ENGAGEMENT STRATEGY

Common tactics used to drive engagement:

### Comment Triggers
The "Comment [KEYWORD] for [FREE THING]" pattern is the single most used engagement tactic in this dataset. **42 of 86 posts (49%) use it.** The formula:

- **Keyword is always 1 word, all caps or quoted**: "BANANA", "STACK", "CLONE", "REPLICATE", "AI", "PROMPT", "skills", "SCRIPT", "FAST", "REALISM", "WIZARD", "Hooks", "GUIDE", "VIDEO", "YouTube", "SHORT", "SEND", "CHALLENGE", "KLING", "data", "CLAUDE", "eBook", "MILLION", "agent", "TRAIN", "group"
- **The reward is always a free digital asset**: prompts, blueprints, PDFs, tool links, tutorials, reference packs, guides, niche digests
- **Auto-DM bots** handle delivery (ManyChat or similar). The reply "@username Done!" confirms automation is running.
- **Average comment count for posts using this pattern**: ~5,847 comments (vs. ~780 for posts without it)

### Save-Worthy Content
Posts that drive saves share these traits:
- Step-by-step tutorials with numbered steps (save as reference)
- Tool lists and comparisons (save to try later)
- Prompt libraries and exact copy-paste text
- "Steal these hooks" style listicles (@iammattjupp, @soravjain)

### Share Triggers
Posts that drive shares (high like-to-comment ratios) use:
- **Identity content**: "Life is unfair. Use your unfair advantages" (@im_roy_lee) — people share what they want to be seen as
- **"Tag someone who needs this"**: implicit in content that solves a universal problem
- **Spectacle content**: visually stunning AI demos that make people say "you need to see this" (@nordic_scott, @jornadatop)
- **Relatable humor**: @andrewandsilvia (91K likes) and @leaflings.app (23K likes) use couple/lifestyle humor

How these creators connect content to revenue:

### Products/Services Mentioned

- **SaaS/Tool affiliates**: @__diditee__ promotes Higgsfield.ai, @joshhoole promotes invideo.io, @winkguys promotes Lovart.ai, @nivedan.ai promotes Emergentlabs/Clawdbot, @ineffable_ai22 promotes arcads_ai

- **Challenges/Events**: @jeremyleeminer runs a 3-day challenge (with a Rolex giveaway as bait)
- **Waitlists**: @_suhaila19_ pushes a Keyframe AI waitlist (43,971 comments on a single waitlist post)
- **eBooks**: @nordic_scott sells an eBook on video editing

### Funnel Structure
The dominant funnel across all 72 creators is:

**Free Reel (hook) --> Comment keyword --> Auto-DM free resource --> Nurture sequence --> Paid offer**

This 4-step funnel appears in at least 42 of the 86 posts. The free resource (lead magnet) is always:
- A PDF with prompts
- A tool link or tutorial
- A blueprint or step-by-step guide
- Access to a free community or challenge

The paid offer sits behind the DM sequence and is rarely mentioned in the post itself.

### Soft Sell Patterns
- **Results-first selling**: @scottsmithyta leads with "$114,224 last month" then casually offers the blueprint. The income claim IS the pitch.
- **Tool demonstration as affiliate play**: @__diditee__ never says "buy Higgsfield" — he shows stunning results, then the tool mention feels like a helpful recommendation.
- **"I built this, you can too"**: @timkoda_, @rebecca_adehill, @kayvon.ai show their personal workflow, then offer the guide. The sale is framed as sharing, not selling.
- **Social proof CTA**: @brownietrds shows "first course buyer" — the celebration post IS the ad.
