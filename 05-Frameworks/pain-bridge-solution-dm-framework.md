## THE FRAMEWORK

Most people open cold DMs/calls by saying what they built. That's pitching. This framework makes the prospect feel their own pain first, so they show up thinking "I'm here to solve MY problem", not "I'm here to get pitched."

### Step 1: Paint the Pain
"You know how [their type of company/person] often [specific problem they experience]? But because [reason they can't fix it themselves], they end up [consequence / lost money / missed opportunity]..."

### Step 2: Bridge to Your Solution
"So what we do is [your solution], that way [specific outcome they want]."

### Step 3: Quantify the Win
"That way they're typically [saving/gaining X per month]."

### Step 4: Qualify with a Question (extract MORE pain)
"Just to see if it would even be helpful for you, [question about their current situation]?"

### Step 5: Stack the Pain with Math
When they answer, do the math out loud:
"So that's like $X/month in [revenue you're missing / time you're wasting]?"

### Step 6: Transition
"Well here's what I can do to help you out..."

## WHY IT WORKS

- The prospect nods along the whole time because you're describing THEIR reality
- By the time you mention your solution, they already want it
- The qualifying questions make THEM articulate the pain (they convince themselves)
- The math makes the cost of NOT acting feel real
- They show up to the sales call thinking "I need to solve this" not "I'm getting pitched"

## RULES
- NEVER lead with "I built X" or "We offer Y"
- Lead with the pain they feel. Always.
- The pain scenario must be SPECIFIC to their industry/role
- Ask questions, don't tell. Let them discover the gap.
- Keep each message under 100 words
- No em dashes. Conversational tone. Read it out loud.

## EXAMPLE FROM THE VIDEO (AI Receptionist)

"You know how a lot of local businesses have people call them like 2am, but because they don't really have staff in place at that time, they're not really able to pick up and then sometimes they lose out on potential deals? So what we do is we actually build AI receptionist for them, that way 24 hours a day they have somebody picking up the phone and scheduling in every single person who calls. That way they're typically saving at least 3-5 clients a month. But just to see if it would even be helpful for you, who do you have picking up the phone now?"
