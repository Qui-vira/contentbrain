# Content Patterns: Multi-Creator Hook Study (Instagram)

> Analyzed 28 posts from 17 creators on 2026-04-01 | Platform: Instagram

## Format Performance

| Format | Count | Avg Engagement | Best For |
|--------|-------|----------------|----------|
| Reels | 25 (89%) | ~62K avg | Tutorials, demos, story hooks, talking head advice |
| Carousels | 3 (11%) | ~20K avg | Hook education, save-worthy frameworks, step-by-step guides |

Reels dominate this dataset 9:1. Every post above 30K engagement is a Reel. Carousels appear only from strategy/education creators (@thepostprotocol, @soravjain) where the goal is saves over views.

## ManyChat/DM Automation Analysis

This is the standout pattern. 39% of all posts use comment-trigger DM automation, and it appears across every niche in the dataset.

### How Each Creator Implements It

**@karenxcheng (4 posts, 4 different keywords)**
- Uses a unique keyword per post in her series: SETTINGS, OUTFIT, CREATOR, GIANT
- Each keyword delivers a different resource (preset pack, outfit links, tool list, tutorial)
- This is the most sophisticated implementation: serial content where each episode has its own keyword, training the audience to comment on every new post
- Combined engagement across 4 posts: ~235K

**@thepostprotocol (2 keywords: Hooks, Proven)**
- Education-focused: the DM delivers a hook swipe file or framework PDF
- Doubles up with follow + share CTAs in the same post
- Uses the DM resource as a lead magnet for course/community upsell

**@johnbucog (keyword: Wan)**
- Tutorial creator who delivers the exact AI tool/preset used in the demo
- Single keyword, single resource — simple and effective
- 170K engagement proves tutorials + DM automation is a lethal combo

**@__diditee__ (keyword: BANANA)**
- Memorable, unrelated keyword creates curiosity ("why BANANA?")
- Delivers the 3D tracking template shown in the tutorial
- The absurd keyword itself becomes a micro-hook in the comments

**@timkoda_ (keyword: TRAIN)**
- AI tool demo with the keyword delivering the prompt or workflow
- Lower engagement (~8K) suggests the keyword alone does not drive numbers — the content quality matters first

**@jade.got.curious (keyword: Blueprint DM)**
- Uses "DM me Blueprint" instead of comment keyword — pushes to DMs directly
- More personal, higher intent, but lower volume than comment automation

**@tobi.the.og (keyword: ship)**
- AI tool creator using the keyword to deliver beta access or waitlist link
- Turns content into a product distribution channel

### The Pattern
Comment keyword DM automation serves three functions simultaneously:
1. **Algorithm boost** — mass comments signal engagement, pushing reach
2. **Lead capture** — every commenter becomes a DM contact for future sequences
3. **Value delivery** — the resource creates reciprocity and trust

The creators with the highest engagement (Karen, John, thepostprotocol) all use this pattern. It is not optional for Instagram growth in 2026 — it is table stakes.

## Visual Hook Patterns

### Cinematic/Storytelling Visual (highest performance)
- @humphreytalks: Opens with a dramatic, film-quality scene — dark lighting, slow motion, atmospheric sound design. The visual IS the hook. No text overlay needed.
- @npcfaizan: Casual but aesthetic one-take with strong eye contact and confident body language. The person is the visual hook.
- Performance: 106K-292K engagement. This style rewards production quality and on-screen presence.

### Demo-First Visual (most common)
- @johnbucog, @__diditee__, @karenxcheng: Opens with the finished result (the B-roll shot, the 3D tracking effect, the edit) in the first 1-3 seconds, THEN teaches how.
- The "after" comes before the "before" — this is critical. Showing the process first kills retention.
- Performance: 33K-170K engagement.

### Text Overlay Hook (education creators)
- @thepostprotocol, @soravjain: Bold text on screen in the first frame, usually the hook itself ("These hooks got me 10M views").
- Works for carousel-style education but underperforms compared to visual-first approaches.
- Performance: 15K-40K engagement.

### Tool/Screen Recording Visual
- @timkoda_, @tobi.the.og, @sala.criativa: Opens with a screen recording of the AI tool or editing software in action.
- Lowest visual appeal but serves the tutorial niche. Works when the output is genuinely impressive.
- Performance: 5K-15K engagement.

## Key Takeaways

- **DM automation is mandatory.** 39% of posts use it, and they consistently outperform posts without it. @big_quiv must set up ManyChat comment-trigger flows for every content piece that promotes a resource, tool, or framework. This is the single highest-leverage move from this study.

- **The visual hook beats the text hook on Instagram.** The top 3 performers all led with cinematic or demo visuals, not text overlays. For @big_quiv's Reels: open with the most visually striking moment (a chart move, an AI generation result, a dramatic scene) and save the explanation for second 3+.

- **Series content creates compounding engagement.** @karenxcheng's "Day X" format with unique keywords per episode is a growth machine. @big_quiv should launch a named series (e.g., "Day X of Web3 signals that actually hit") with a new comment keyword each episode.

- **The top creators never sell in the content itself.** Monetization happens in the DM sequence, the bio link, or the resource delivered after the comment keyword. The content stays pure value. @big_quiv should separate the content layer (education/story) from the sales layer (DM automation/bio funnel).

- **Casual authenticity outperforms polish in talking-head content.** @npcfaizan (106K) and @jessijeanhome (48K) both use raw, one-take delivery with zero editing. Over-produced talking heads underperform. When @big_quiv does face-to-camera content, keep it real — no teleprompter energy.
