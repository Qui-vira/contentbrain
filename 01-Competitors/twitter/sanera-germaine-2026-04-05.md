## Writing Style -- Core Patterns

### Hook Types (Ranked by Performance)
1. **Cinematic scene-drop** -- drops into a specific visual memory, no intro
   - *"When I was taking my WAEC few years ago, this pretty babe came to the exam hall without a pencil..."*
2. **Confession + Twist** -- opens with vulnerability, flips on reader for humor/insight
   - *"I got humbled so Bad. Someone I know learnt a skill... course fee was N1000. I lost interest immediately."*
3. **Bold earned brag** -- a single number or claim, immediately humanized
   - *"I made $10,000 in 3 weeks this January 2024."*
4. **Participation hook** -- asks audience into the journey in real time
   - *"Learning how to manage 50k for a month. Can I do it? Y'all wish me luck."*

### Sentence Rhythm
- Staircase structure: 1-2 sentences per paragraph, max
- Short emotional opener > longer setup > punchline/twist > emotional payoff
- Never concludes with a lecture. Ends on energy, a question, or an insight the reader has to sit with

### Tone Markers
- Nigerian internet dialect: "oooo", "Broooo", "Ahhhh", "shiii", "na water"
- Web3 hybrid: "LFG", "OG", "giga-", "Matrix", "hacked"
- Emojis used sparingly but precisely: vulnerable self-deprecation, shared humor, direct challenge, earnest ask
- Numbers formatted with apostrophes: $10'000, 20'000. Distinct personal style.

### Emotional Arc Per Post
```
Relatability > Tension/Humor > Aspiration/Lesson
```
Never skips the middle. The tension/humor step is what makes the lesson land.

## Strategic Patterns to Study

### The Quote-Tweet Amplification Strategy
Her two highest-view posts (499K, 353K) are quote tweets onto trending conversations. She does NOT debate. She storytells her position in one punchy scene. The original post's traffic does the distribution; her story does the persuasion.

**Template extracted:**

> Sanera's reply: [Specific personal memory or scene that proves her stance in 1-3 lines, ending with an emotional kicker]

### The Vulnerability Loop
She regularly reveals past broke/struggling self to build identity bridges with her audience:
- Past: "I was super broke", "less than 500 followers", "borrowed from loan apps"
- Present: $10K months, 21.9K followers, KOL Manager role
- Future: actively growing, publicly tracking, learning in public

This loop is **never pity-seeking**. It's always reframed as fuel ("I refuse to take this as my reality / been giga desperate ever since").

## Why She Goes Viral -- TL;DR

1. **Scene over statement.** She never tells you what to think. She drops you into a moment and lets you feel it. The WAEC pencil story is 499K views not because of the opinion but because of the cinematography of the memory.
2. **The delayed math.** Her biggest posts withhold the calculation or punchline until you've already scrolled through the emotional setup. By the time the number lands, you're invested.
3. **Vulnerability as authority.** She doesn't build authority by positioning herself as an expert. She builds it by being the most honest person in the room about her own journey. When she brags now, it lands harder because you watched her crawl.
4. **The cultural specificity trap.** Nigerian slang + web3 jargon + real Naira amounts + specific local references (asoebi dress, WAEC exams, loan apps) makes her content feel like it was written for her exact audience. But the emotions are universal. That's the trap: local enough to feel personal, universal enough to travel.
5. **The controversy engine.** Every thread has a landmine. An opinion that will make half the audience say "EXACTLY" and the other half say "wait, what?" That split drives the replies, which drives the reach.
