## EPISODE 1 — Claude: Trade Journal Analysis

> **Content Type:** VALUE (with Personality hook on Instagram/TikTok) | Goal: Authority + Leads | Structure: Tactical | Slot: Morning
> **Audit note:** TYPE FLAGGED. X/Twitter hook ("You can ask Claude...") is clean VALUE. Instagram hook ("I uploaded my trade journal...") and TikTok hook ("Watch me upload...") use first-person framing — this makes them read as PERSONALITY on those platforms but the body is VALUE instruction. DECISION: No split. Using personal experience as the opening hook into a VALUE teaching piece is a legitimate and effective technique. The body (use case + step demo) is fully VALUE. Label stands as VALUE with Personality hook on IG/TikTok. The distinction: Big Quiv's experience is the credibility device that earns the right to teach, not the lesson itself.

**Publish:** April 9, 2026 (Day 9)
**Primary segments:** Beginners, Developers, Traders

**X/Twitter hook:**
"You can ask Claude to scan your last 30 trades and tell you your worst habit. Most traders don't know this is possible. Here's how."

**Instagram hook:**
"I uploaded my trade journal to Claude. It found a pattern I had been missing for 6 months."

**TikTok hook:**
"Watch me upload 30 trades to Claude. What it found changed my trading." — First frame: spreadsheet drop into Claude.

**The use case:**
Claude reads your trade CSV and identifies: worst time of day to trade, most consistent losing setup, best R:R ratio pairs, emotional patterns (e.g. revenge trading after 2 consecutive losses). Bro what. Behavioural analytics that used to require a coach.

**The demo (try this now):**
1. Export your trades from your broker (CSV or copy-paste into a table)
2. Open Claude. Paste trades. Prompt: "Analyse these trades. Tell me: (a) my worst trading habit, (b) my best-performing setup, (c) time of day I perform worst, (d) emotional patterns you can infer."
3. Claude returns structured analysis with observations and actionable rules
4. Optional: "Write me 5 trading rules based on what you found."

**Tools needed:** Claude (web or API, free tier), trade journal CSV, browser

**Course lead-in:**
"In Module 1 of the AI Automation course, Developer Track, we build this as a full web app: upload your broker CSV, Claude analyses automatically, you get a weekly report in your inbox."

## EPISODE 4 — Remotion: Batch Video Generation from CSV

> **Content Type:** VALUE | Goal: Authority + Leads | Structure: Tactical/Demo | Slot: Morning
> **Audit note:** "You can use Remotion to generate 30 videos from a CSV file" is clean VALUE. TikTok hook "Watch me generate 30 videos in one terminal command" is demo-style. The body is a step-by-step build. No type conflict. Label: VALUE.

**Publish:** April 30, 2026 (Day 30 — final day of launch window)
**Primary segments:** Developers, Creators

**X/Twitter hook:**
"You can use Remotion to generate 30 videos from a CSV file. One command. No manual editing. Here's the exact script."

**Instagram hook:**
"I generated 30 videos from a spreadsheet. Automatically. No editing. One command." — First frame: the CSV. Last frame: folder of 30 MP4 files.

**TikTok hook:**
"Watch me generate 30 videos in one terminal command." — First frame: terminal. Show render progress bar. Last frame: folder full of videos.

**The use case:**
Remotion renders videos programmatically from React components. Combined with a CSV of topics and Claude-written content, you generate dozens of unique branded videos in one automated run. 30 rows = 30 videos. At this point, no editor on the planet beats that speed. Use cases: daily tip videos, product videos, platform-adapted content at scale.

**The demo (try this now):**
1. Create CSV: columns = title, hook, body_text, cta, bg_color
2. Build a Remotion composition (or use the course template)
3. Claude writes the Remotion component code mapping CSV columns to video elements
4. Run: `npx remotion render --props='{"csv":"your-data.csv"}' MyComposition out/`
5. Remotion generates one MP4 per CSV row. 30 rows = 30 videos.

**Tools needed:** Node.js 18+, Remotion CLI, Claude API (to write component code), CSV

**Course lead-in:**
"In Module 4 of Coding with AI, Developer Track, we build the full pipeline: CSV → Claude scripts → fal.ai images → Kling video → MiniMax voice → Remotion assembly. 5 complete videos, one command."
