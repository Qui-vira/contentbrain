# Outlier AI: Complete Registration and Earning Guide (2026)

---

## 1. What is Outlier?

Outlier AI is a platform operated by **Scale AI** (valued at $14 billion) that pays freelancers to train artificial intelligence models. You evaluate, correct, and improve AI-generated responses — essentially teaching AI to be smarter.

- **Parent company:** Scale AI (founded by Alexandr Wang, San Francisco)
- **Total paid out:** $100M+ to 40,000+ contributors globally
- **Domains:** 40+ (writing, coding, STEM, languages, audio, law, medicine)
- **Relationship to Remotasks:** Both are Scale AI subsidiaries. Outlier handles LLM/text training. Remotasks handles computer vision/image labeling.

---

## 2. What Do You Actually Do on Outlier?

You get paid to make AI smarter. The tasks include:

| Task Type | What You Do | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Response Evaluation** | Compare 2+ AI answers, pick the better one, explain why | "Which response to 'explain quantum computing' is more accurate?" |
| **Content Rewriting** | Fix AI-generated text for accuracy, clarity, structure | Correct a factual error in an AI's explanation of blockchain |
| **Coding Tasks** | Review AI code, debug errors, suggest improvements | Fix a Python function the AI wrote incorrectly |
| **Fact-Checking** | Verify AI claims against real data | Check if the AI's statistics about crypto market cap are correct |
| **Prompt Engineering** | Write questions designed to test AI limits | Create a tricky math problem that would confuse ChatGPT |
| **Data Annotation** | Label and categorize content for training data | Tag whether a response is "helpful," "harmful," or "neutral" |
| **Specialized Review** | Domain expert review in law, medicine, math, science | Verify an AI's legal analysis of a contract clause |

**Bottom line:** If you can read, think critically, and write clearly — you can do this work.

## 3. Requirements

### Must-Have
- **Age:** 18+
- **Education:** Associate degree minimum (bachelor's or higher preferred)
- **Valid government ID:** Passport or driver's license (not expired)
- **Resume:** Up to date, reflecting real skills and experience
- **LinkedIn profile:** Connected and current
- **Equipment:** Laptop/desktop with stable internet connection
- **English:** Strong proficiency (for platform navigation; some projects are in other languages)

### Nice-to-Have (Gets You Higher Pay)
- Graduate degree (Master's, PhD)
- Professional certifications
- STEM background (math, physics, chemistry, biology, computer science)
- Coding skills (Python, JavaScript, Java, C++, Go, TypeScript)
- Legal or medical expertise
- Published academic work or GitHub portfolio

### NOT Required
- Previous AI training experience
- AI/ML engineering skills
- Specific software knowledge

## 4. Step-by-Step Registration Process

### Step 1: Go to outlier.ai
Visit **https://app.outlier.ai/en/expert/signup** and click "Create an Account."

**Tip from @Fra_nkXBT:** "Creating an account on Outlier is one thing — passing the onboarding quest is a whole different game." Prepare properly before starting.

### Step 2: Create Your Account
- Sign up with email or Google account
- Enter: full name, phone number, date of birth, country, address

**⚠️ Nigeria Users:** The platform rejects Nigerian (+234) phone numbers. You need:
- A **paid VPN** (not free) to appear as US/UK
- A **US virtual phone number** (TextNow, Google Voice via VPN)
- OR an **RDP** (Remote Desktop Protocol) — preferred by @ObaDeleke: "I'll suggest RDP over VPN" because it gives a real US machine fingerprint, not just an IP change
- Recommended proxies: **NSocks, CleanLTE** (from @sleektru)

### Step 3: Complete Your Profile
- Upload your **current resume** (the system auto-extracts skills from it)
- Connect your **LinkedIn profile** (cross-referenced for credibility)
- Technical skills validated via GitHub, Google Scholar, or academic email if applicable

**Tip:** Be honest. "Authentic expertise always performs better than inflated claims." — Outlier's onboarding engineer

### Step 4: Identity Verification
Outlier uses **Persona** (same service banks use). You'll need to:
- Choose your ID type (passport, driver's license, state ID)
- Take clear photos of ID front and back
- Take a live selfie (well-lit, face centered)
- Verify your phone via SMS

**Common failure reasons:**
- Expired ID
- Blurry photos
- ID details don't match profile info exactly
- Using someone else's ID

**You cannot get paid until this step is completed.** Processing takes 1-2 business days.

### Step 5: Select Your Skills
- Choose a **maximum of 10 skills**
- Only pick skills you can genuinely demonstrate — you WILL be tested
- High-demand skills in 2026: advanced mathematics, coding (Python, JavaScript), legal analysis, medical reasoning, data science, linguistics, technical writing

### Step 6: Take the Qualification Test
- **General Reasoning Screening** — you must score **80% or higher**
- Tests analytical thinking, reading comprehension, logical reasoning
- Some projects require additional domain-specific assessments
- **This is often ONE attempt only** — prepare carefully

**Tip from @ObaDeleke:** "Very few people pass the onboarding stage." Take your time, don't rush.

**⚠️ April 2026 Update:** @Rddixcrypt reports that the Aether assessment exam is **temporarily suspended** for new registrations. If you can't access the assessment, check back regularly or pivot to coding-related tasks on other platforms. [Unverified, from single source]

### Step 7: Start Earning
- Once approved, you'll see available projects on your dashboard
- Each listing shows: task description, expected time, pay rate
- Accept tasks that match your verified skills
- Your first project may include unpaid or lower-paid training

**Timeline:** Active onboarding = 30-90 minutes. Getting first project = 1 day to several weeks.

### Tiered Payment Structure
Some roles use tiered payments:
- **Tier 1:** Starts ~$15/hr (new contributors)
- **Tier 2+:** Advances based on performance and accuracy

## 6. Payment Methods

### Available Options
| Method | Availability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **PayPal** | Global (most common) | Works in Nigeria via domiciliary account |
| **Airtm** | Global | Good for African users, converts to local currency |
| **ACH Bank Transfer** | US only | Direct deposit to US bank account |

### Payment Schedule
- **Processed:** Every **Tuesday**
- **For work done:** Previous Tuesday through Monday (midnight UTC)
- **Minimum payout:** $10 (current; some older sources say $50)
- **Funds arrive:** Within 1-3 business days after processing

### For Nigerian Users
- **PayPal** → withdraw to a **domiciliary (USD) account** at your Nigerian bank to avoid conversion losses
- **Airtm** → alternative that lets you convert to Naira or withdraw to local bank
- Set up your payment method BEFORE you start working
- Double-check that your withdrawal pipeline actually works (do a test with a small amount)

## 8. Common Mistakes to Avoid

### During Registration
| Mistake | What Happens | How to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Claiming skills you can't demonstrate | Fail assessment, locked out of good projects | Only select skills you're truly comfortable with |
| Using expired or blurry ID | Verification fails, delays by days | Check ID expiry, take photos in good lighting |
| Profile info doesn't match ID | Verification rejected | Make name, DOB, address match exactly |
| Rushing through reasoning test | Score below 80%, no retake | Take your time, there's no speed bonus |
| Not having LinkedIn ready | Incomplete profile, lower priority | Set up LinkedIn before starting |

### While Working
| Mistake | What Happens | How to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Prioritizing speed over accuracy | Quality score drops, account banned | @iamveektoria_: "Mess up too much and they'll kick you out" |
| Ignoring project guidelines | Flagged by quality review, restricted | Read every instruction before starting |
| Using ChatGPT/AI tools to complete tasks | Detected and banned immediately | Do all work yourself |
| Not checking for new tasks regularly | Miss high-paying projects | Check dashboard daily |
| Treating it as guaranteed income | Disappointment and financial stress | It's supplemental income, not a salary |
| Letting large balance accumulate | Risk losing money if account deactivated | Withdraw earnings regularly |

### Platform Risks to Know
- **Silent removal:** Workers report being locked out without notification or explanation
- **Automated quality reviews:** Can be "bugged and subjective" — a single poor review can flag your account
- **No human escalation:** If flagged, there's often no human manager to hear you out
- **Unpaid training:** New project onboarding can be 1-2 hours and unpaid. If you fail the assessment after training, you get nothing.

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Outlier AI legit?**
A: Yes. It's operated by Scale AI (valued at $14 billion). $100M+ paid to contributors. Multiple Nigerian users confirm receiving payments.

**Q: Can I use Outlier from Nigeria?**
A: Yes, but with workarounds. Nigerian phone numbers are rejected. You need a VPN/RDP and US virtual number. Yoruba and Hausa language projects are specifically available for Nigerians.

**Q: How long does it take to start earning?**
A: Onboarding takes 30-90 minutes. ID verification takes 1-2 days. Getting your first project can take days to weeks.

**Q: What's the minimum payout?**
A: $10 (current). Payments processed every Tuesday.

**Q: Can I work on my phone?**
A: Most tasks require a laptop/desktop. Phone is needed for verification only.

**Q: What if I fail the reasoning test?**
A: You may not get a retake. Prepare thoroughly before attempting it. Score 80%+ required.

**Q: Can my account be banned?**
A: Yes. Low accuracy, using AI tools, or violating guidelines can result in immediate bans with no warning or appeal process.

**Q: Is it worth it for Nigerians?**
A: Mixed opinions. @ObaDeleke (2,058 likes): Shows earnings proof and recommends it. @remotemondays (270 likes): Says "not worth the risk" for Africans. The consensus: worth trying if you have the right skills and can handle the setup, but don't rely on it as sole income.

**Q: How does Outlier compare to Remotasks?**
A: Both are owned by Scale AI. Outlier pays more ($15-$100/hr vs $1-5/task) but is harder to get into. Remotasks is better for beginners with faster onboarding.

**Q: What should I do if Aether assessment is suspended?**
A: As of April 2026, some users report the assessment is temporarily paused. Check back regularly, or pivot to coding tasks on other platforms (DataAnnotation, Mindrift, Remotasks).
