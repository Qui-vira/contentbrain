## What "Commodity" Status Actually Means

When the SEC classifies a token as a commodity instead of a security, five things change overnight:

**1. No more securities lawsuits.**
The SEC can no longer sue exchanges or projects for listing these tokens. That legal cloud that's been hanging over crypto since 2020? Gone for these 16.

**2. Exchanges can list freely.**
Coinbase, Kraken, Robinhood. They can list, promote, and offer trading pairs for these tokens without fear of enforcement action.

**3. Institutions can buy without legal risk.**
Hedge funds, pension funds, and asset managers now have regulatory clarity to allocate to these tokens. This unlocks billions in potential inflows.

**4. Staking is officially cleared.**
This is the part nobody is talking about. The filing explicitly states that staking rewards and airdrop distributions under a commodity framework do not trigger securities laws. Projects can now distribute rewards freely.

**5. ETF products get the green light.**
BlackRock already launched a staked ETH ETF (ticker: ETHB) on March 12. Expect more products for SOL, AVAX, and others in Q2 2026.
