# ICT Smart Money Rules

> Quivira's ICT/Smart Money trading methodology. Used by /technical-analyst to identify and score setups.

---

## Order Blocks

- **Definition:** The last bearish candle before a bullish impulse move (bullish OB), or the last bullish candle before a bearish impulse move (bearish OB).
- **Valid when:** Price returns to the OB zone and shows rejection (wick, engulfing candle, or volume spike).
- **Invalidated when:** Price closes through the OB with displacement (strong momentum candle).
- **Best on:** 1H, 4H, Daily timeframes. Lower timeframes for entry refinement only.

## Fair Value Gaps (FVG)

- **Definition:** A 3-candle pattern where a gap exists between candle 1's high and candle 3's low (bullish FVG) or candle 1's low and candle 3's high (bearish FVG).
- **Trade rule:** Price tends to return to fill the FVG before continuing in the impulse direction.
- **High-probability FVGs:** Those that form during kill zones or align with order blocks.
- **Invalidated when:** Price fills the FVG and continues through it without reversal.

## Market Structure Shift (MSS) / Break of Structure (BOS)

- **MSS:** A break of the most recent swing high/low WITH displacement (strong candle, not a wick). Signals potential trend reversal.
- **BOS:** A break of structure in the direction of the existing trend. Signals trend continuation.
- **Rule:** Only trade in the direction confirmed by the most recent MSS/BOS on your execution timeframe.
- **Confirmation:** MSS must be followed by a pullback into an OB or FVG for a valid entry.

## Liquidity Sweeps

- **Definition:** Price takes out a previous swing high/low (where stop losses cluster), then reverses.
- **Bullish sweep:** Price dips below a previous low, sweeps the liquidity, then reverses up.
- **Bearish sweep:** Price spikes above a previous high, sweeps the liquidity, then reverses down.
- **Rule:** Wait for the sweep to complete and confirmation (MSS or rejection candle) before entering. Never enter during the sweep.

## Kill Zones (Session Times)

- **London Kill Zone:** 2:00 AM - 5:00 AM EST — Highest probability for Forex setups. Often sets the daily high or low.
- **New York Kill Zone:** 7:00 AM - 10:00 AM EST — Highest probability for crypto and Forex. Often reverses or extends the London move.
- **Asia Kill Zone:** 7:00 PM - 9:00 PM EST — Lower volatility. Consolidation zone. Setups are valid but lower confidence.
- **Rule:** Prioritize entries during London and New York kill zones. Asia setups require extra confluence.

## Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)

- **Definition:** Entry in the 62%-79% Fibonacci retracement zone of an impulse move.
- **How to use:** After an MSS or BOS, wait for a pullback. Draw Fibonacci from the swing low to swing high (or vice versa). Enter between the 0.618 and 0.786 levels.
- **Best when:** OTE zone overlaps with an order block or FVG.
- **Stop loss:** Below/above the swing point used for the Fibonacci drawing.

## Setup Validation Checklist

Before signaling any trade, ALL of these must be true:

1. Higher timeframe bias is clear (Daily/4H trend direction established)
2. Kill zone timing is active OR setup has 5+ confluences
3. At least one ICT concept present (OB, FVG, liquidity sweep, MSS/BOS)
4. Minimum 3 total confluences (ICT + indicators combined)
5. Risk-to-reward is minimum 1:2
6. No major news event within 30 minutes of entry
