# Content Creation Rules (Quivira Method)

## Hook Rules
- The first line decides everything. If the hook is weak, nothing after it matters.
- Use the Callout-Flex-Reveal formula: call out a specific audience, flex authority or proof, reveal the value.
- Hooks must create tension, curiosity, or confrontation. Neutral hooks get scrolled past.
- Start with "you" or a bold claim. Never start with "I" unless telling a personal story that serves the audience.
- Test: "Would I stop scrolling for this?" If no, rewrite.

## Script Rules
- Scripts must be under 125 words for short-form video.
- Every sentence must earn its place. If removing a line does not hurt the message, cut it.
- Use emphasis markers: [PAUSE], [SLOW], [FAST], [EMPHASIS] for voiceover pacing.
- End with one clear CTA. One word, max 5 letters.

## Visual Rules
- First 3 seconds are the most important. The opening scene must be the most visually striking.
- Dark backgrounds, red accents, clean typography. Always.
- Transitions serve the story. Hard cuts for urgency. Dissolves for reflection. Zooms for emphasis. Never decorative.
- Audio and visuals must reinforce each other. If the voice says "chaos," visuals feel chaotic. If the voice says "clarity," visuals feel clean.

## Strategy Rules
- Every piece of content serves one goal: sales, reach, leads, authority, or community. No exceptions.
- Content mix: 30% value, 25% story, 20% community, 15% promo, 10% hot takes.
- Never post without a monetization path. Every piece connects (directly or indirectly) to a product, service, or community offer.
- Data beats opinions. Back claims with on-chain data, engagement metrics, or verifiable facts.
- Trends are only valuable if they connect to the brand. Do not chase trends with no angle for @big_quiv.

## Competitor Rules
- Competitor analysis is about finding gaps. What are they NOT saying? What angle are they missing?
- Never copy. Identify what works, then do it differently and better.
- Study their hooks, not their content. The hook is the only transferable element.

## Execution Rules
- Show output first, explain later. No introductions before delivering what was asked.
- If something fails (API error, missing data, unclear brief), say what failed and suggest a fix. Never stop silently.
- Never produce filler content. If you cannot write something excellent, ask for more context.
