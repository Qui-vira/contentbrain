## PRE-LAUNCH HOOKS (Days 8–16)

> **Audit note — Section relabeled.** "Authority" was the goal, not the content type. Hooks in this section are a mix of types: PERSONALITY ("I've been building..."), HOT TAKES ("You don't need to know how to code" is a bold claim), COMMUNITY ("Be honest: what's the #1 thing..."). Content type labeled per hook in the Type column below.
>
> **TYPE CONFLICT FOUND:** "I've been building something for 30 days. Here's what it is." — this hook starts as PERSONALITY (about Big Quiv) but the implied body teaches what he built (VALUE). DECISION: Hook is PERSONALITY. The post body determines actual content type. Flag to ghostwriter: if body teaches features/how-to, the post becomes VALUE, not Personality.

## SOCIAL PROOF HOOKS (Days 23–28)

> **Content Type:** TYPE FLAGGED — split decision needed.
> - "First 5 students are in. Here's what they said when they saw the curriculum." → PROMO (social proof in service of a sale)
> - "Week 1 inside the Quivira AI Course — what we built in 7 days." → VALUE (showing what was built teaches something) OR PROMO (if the post is a course ad). DECISION: Label as VALUE if body shows actual builds. PROMO if body is "join now." Ghostwriter must decide at write time.
> - "One student automated their entire client onboarding in under 90 minutes. Zero prior AI experience." → VALUE (case study of a result that teaches what's possible) with PROMO tail (CTA to join). No split needed. Label: VALUE with PROMO CTA.

## USAGE NOTES
- Never use the same hook on back-to-back days on the same platform
- Money proof hooks: always include verification level in post body ("industry range" or "composite example")
- Announcement hooks: pair with a clear CTA (DM COURSE, Link in bio, DM [keyword])
- Validation hooks (Day 15): monitor replies as market research. Adjust messaging based on what surfaces. Say less if the replies are cold, more if they're warm.
