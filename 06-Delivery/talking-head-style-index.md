# Talking Head Style Index — Delivery Modes That Feel Real

> **Source of truth for how Big Quiv appears on camera.** Video skills read this when specifying delivery notes.
> Last rebuilt: 2026-04-01 | Total styles: 52

---

## The Problem This Solves

AI video tools default to "man stands still, talks to camera, medium shot." That looks fake because real creators vary their energy, framing, and movement constantly. This index forces variety.

---

## Selection Algorithm

When a skill specifies delivery style for a video:

1. **MATCH** energy level to content emotion (angry topic = high energy, teaching topic = calm authority)
2. **PICK** a camera setup that fits the visual hook chosen from visual-hook-index.md
3. **SPECIFY** at least 2 shot changes within a 30-second video (static = death)
4. **ROTATE** styles. Do not use the same camera + energy combo within 5 days.

## PACING RULES (applied to every video)

1. **Visual change every 2-3 seconds** — no static shots longer than 3 seconds without a cut, zoom, text, or B-roll change
2. **Pattern interrupt every 5-8 seconds** — change something unexpected to reset viewer attention
3. **New sentence = new cut** — sync shot changes to voiceover beats
4. **First frame as standalone thumbnail** — must work as a still image that stops the scroll
5. **SFX on at least 60% of shots** — silence is intentional, not default
6. **Bass drop / impact SFX at every hard cut** — audio reinforces visual rhythm
7. **Minimum 2 shot changes per 30 seconds** — static = death for retention
